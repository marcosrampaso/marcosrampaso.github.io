Wildfire Prediction in Brazil's Central-West Region

This project investigates the relationship between climatic, spatial, and socioeconomic factors to predict wildfire occurrences in Brazil’s Central-West region. The analysis integrates geospatial visualization, feature engineering, and machine learning models.

The interactive map highlights municipalities with the highest agricultural gross value added (GVA), revealing strong spatial concentration of economic activity in specific regions, which are relevant for wildfire risk modeling.

This map displays the top 100 municipalities with the highest Fire Radiative Power (FRP) values, indicating areas with significant wildfire activity. The spatial distribution of these fires provides insights into regions most affected by wildfires.

The crossed data map combines wildfire occurrences (Fire Radiative Power – FRP) with agricultural economic activity (Gross Value Added – GVA) to explore spatial correlations between fire events and economic factors.

  • Blue pins: Central-West region
  • Pink pins: South region
  • Orange pins: Northeast region
  • Green pins: North region
  • Red pins: Southeast region

The visualization reveals that several municipalities with high wildfire occurrences also exhibit significant agricultural economic activity. Notably, Brazil’s Central-West region shows a strong spatial overlap between high Fire Radiative Power (FRP) and elevated agricultural Gross Value Added (GVA), reinforcing the hypothesis that intensive agricultural production is closely associated with increased wildfire incidence. Based on these findings, the Central-West region was selected for further analysis and predictive modeling.

Analysis of the Central-West Region

Focusing on Brazil’s Central-West region, this stage of the project investigates the relationship between wildfire activity and key climatic, spatial, and socioeconomic variables. The analysis incorporates meteorological data — including daily precipitation and average daily Fire Radiative Power (FRP) — to better understand temporal patterns and potential drivers of wildfire occurrences.

Climatic variables such as temperature, precipitation, humidity, and wind speed were obtained from meteorological stations and made available through the Kaggle dataset Hourly Weather Surface Brazil Southeast Region . These data were aggregated to daily metrics to enable direct comparison with wildfire indicators. After calculating daily precipitation averages, these values were merged with daily FRP data at the municipality level to create a comprehensive dataset for analysis. It can also be observed that wildfire occurrences in the Central-West region are strongly associated with municipalities identified as the driest, suggesting that prolonged periods of low precipitation play a key role in increasing wildfire activity.
