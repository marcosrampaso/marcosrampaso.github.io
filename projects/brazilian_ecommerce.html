Olist Customer Retention Analysis

Project Overview

This project uses the Brazilian E-Commerce Public Dataset by Olist to explore customer retention and identify clients at risk of churn. By analyzing purchase history, order frequency, ticket value, product categories, and reviews, we aim to uncover patterns that predict which customers may stop buying and generate actionable insights to improve retention strategies.

Project Summary

As an ecommerce operations manager, the key question is: "Which customers are at risk of leaving, and what patterns in their behavior indicate potential churn?" This project integrates data cleaning, exploratory data analysis (EDA), feature engineering, and supervised machine learning to predict churn and provide actionable recommendations for improving customer retention.

  • Problem: Losing customers reduces revenue and affects long-term business growth.
  • Data: Real-world Olist dataset with 100k+ orders from 2016 to 2018, including customer, seller, product, payment, shipping, and review information.
  • Approach: End-to-end workflow with EDA, feature engineering, predictive modeling (Random Forest / XGBoost), and visualization.
  • Key Insight: Customer churn is influenced by purchase frequency, delayed deliveries, ticket value, and satisfaction ratings.
  • Result: Predictive model identifies customers at risk of churn with high accuracy, enabling targeted retention strategies.
